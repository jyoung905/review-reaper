# Review Reaper Prospecting Playbook

## Current strategy
Review Reaper is not GTA-limited. GTA was the sandbox. The scalable strategy is niche-first across Canada/US.

## Best initial niches
1. Dental clinics
2. Med spas / cosmetic clinics
3. Auto repair shops
4. Chiropractors / physio clinics
5. Home services

## Qualification criteria
A prospect is worth adding if most of these are true:
- Google/public rating roughly 4.0–4.7.
- Enough reviews to matter: 50+ for med spas, 100+ for dental/auto if possible.
- Recent or specific 1–3 star reviews exist.
- Complaint themes are visible: pricing, communication, rude service, booking, results, refunds, repair quality, billing.
- Business has meaningful customer lifetime value.
- Owner replies are missing, inconsistent, defensive, or generic.
- Official website/contact page or direct email is verifiable.

## Status labels
- `research_ready_needs_evidence`: promising, but needs exact review evidence saved.
- `evidence_verified`: exact review examples and contact source verified.
- `mini_audit_ready`: enough evidence to create a mini-audit report.
- `send_ready_needs_approval`: outreach draft and mini-audit prepared; James must approve before send.
- `sent_followup_due_YYYY-MM-DD`: sent with follow-up date.
- `do_not_contact`: poor fit, bad data, or opted out.

## Outreach rule
Do not send external outreach without current James approval for the batch/content.

## Offer
Lead with free mini-audit, not subscription.

Simple angle:
"I found a few negative review patterns that are probably costing trust. Want me to send over a free mini-audit with response drafts?"

## Monthly product
After signup, Review Reaper scans Google reviews monthly, identifies negative review themes, drafts approval-ready responses, and sends the customer a response pack. The customer posts responses themselves. No auto-posting.
